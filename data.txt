1. Lekki Conservation Centre (LCC)
Name: Lekki Conservation Centre
State / City / LGA: Lagos State / Lekki / Eti-Osa LGA 
Description: A 78-hectare natural resource conservation area covering wetlands and savannah. It is famous for having the longest canopy walkway in Africa.
 Entry fees:
* Canopy Walk: ~₦4,000 - ₦4,500 (Adults)
* Ordinary Entry: ~₦2,500 
Opening hours: Daily, 8:30 AM – 4:30 PM
GPS coordinates: 6.4417° N, 3.5358° E 
Photos:
History: Established in 1990 by the Nigerian Conservation Foundation (NCF) to protect the wildlife and mangrove forests of Nigeria’s South-West coastline from urban sprawl. 
Best time to visit: Dry season (Nov–Mar) to avoid muddy trails; Early morning (around 9 AM) to beat the crowd and heat. 
Safety info: The canopy walk is safe but not recommended for those with a fear of heights. Monkeys are wild—do not feed or touch them.
 Contact info: +234 909 546 0319 (Official NCF line)
 Available activities: Canopy walking, jungle hiking, picnicking, giant chess, seeing monkeys/peacocks, treehouse climbing.
Address / Access Info: KM 19, Lekki-Epe Expressway, Lekki Peninsula II, Lagos 106104, Lagos State, Nigeria

2. Nike Art Gallery
Name: Nike Centre for Art and Culture 
State / City / LGA: Lagos State / Lekki Phase 1 / Eti-Osa LGA 
Description: West Africa's largest art gallery, housed in a magnificent four-story building. It holds thousands of artworks including paintings, textiles, and metalwork. 
Entry fees: Free (Donations or purchases encouraged) 
Opening hours: Daily, 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM 
GPS coordinates: 6.4526° N, 3.4851° E
 Photos:
 History: Founded by Chief Nike Davies-Okundaye, a world-renowned batik and textile artist, to promote Nigerian culture and empower artists. 
Best time to visit: Weekdays for a quieter experience; weekends if you want to meet other art lovers. 
Safety info: Very safe location. Be careful with delicate artworks when moving through narrow aisles. 
Contact info: +234 803 409 6656 
Available activities: Art appreciation, purchasing art, meeting the artist (Chief Nike is often there), textile workshops (by appointment).
Address / Access Info: 2 Nike Art Gallery Road (2 Elegushi/Oba Yekini Elegushi Road), Lekki Phase I, Lekki, Lagos 106104.

4. Freedom Park
Name: Freedom Park Lagos 
State / City / LGA: Lagos State / Lagos Island / Lagos Island LGA 
Description: A memorial and leisure park dedicated to preserving the history and cultural heritage of Nigeria. It was formerly "Her Majesty's Broad Street Prison."
 Entry fees: ~₦1,000 (Higher during concerts/festivals) 
Opening hours: Daily, 11:00 AM – 10:00 PM (Late on concert nights) 
GPS coordinates: 6.4503° N, 3.3934° E 
Photos: History: Originally built in 1882 as a colonial prison. It hosted prominent political prisoners (like Obafemi Awolowo) before being reconstructed into a park in 2010 to commemorate the 50th independence anniversary. 
Best time to visit: Evenings for live band music; December for the "Felabration" or other cultural festivals. 
Safety info: Generally safe, but be cautious when leaving late at night on Lagos Island. Contact info: 0809 500 6567 
Available activities: Live music, museum tour of old prison cells, dining at the food court, photography, theater performances.
Address / Access Info: Old Prison Ground, 1 Hospital Road, adjacent to St. Nicholas Hospital, Lagos Island, Lagos State

5. Kalakuta Republic Museum
Name: Kalakuta Republic Museum 
State / City / LGA: Lagos State / Ikeja / Ikeja LGA
 Description: The former home of Afrobeat legend Fela Kuti, now a museum displaying his clothes, shoes, instruments, and bedroom exactly as he left them.
 Entry fees: ~₦2,000 - ₦5,000 (Prices vary for guided vs. self tours) 
Opening hours: Mon-Sat, 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM 
GPS coordinates: 6.6053° N, 3.3562° E 
Photos:
History: This was Fela’s residence and commune. It was attacked by soldiers in 1977. Fela is buried in the compound. 
Best time to visit: During "Felabration" week (October) or any weekday for a personal tour.
Safety info: Ikeja is busy; stick to the museum grounds and official transport. 
Contact info: +234 802 313 6634 
Available activities: Guided house tour, rooftop bar (chill spot), viewing Fela's tomb, buying souvenirs.
Address / Access Info: 7 Gbemisola Street, Allen, Ikeja 100281, Lagos.

6. Tarkwa Bay Beach
Name: Tarkwa Bay Beach 
State / City / LGA: Lagos State / Tarkwa Bay Island / Eti-Osa LGA 
Description: A sheltered, artificial beach located near the Lagos Harbour. It is accessible only by boat. 
Entry fees:
* Boat Ride: ₦4,000 – ₦6,000 (Return trip from Victoria Island/CMS)
* Gate Fee: ~₦500 - ₦1,000
* Tent/Chair: ₦1,000 – ₦3,000 
Opening hours: Daily, 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM 
GPS coordinates: 6.4036° N, 3.3857° E 
Photos: 
History: Created during the construction of the Lagos harbor moles (breakwaters) to prevent sand silting up the harbor. 
Best time to visit: Weekdays (very quiet/clean); Sunday afternoons (party vibe). 
Safety info: Do not stay past 6:00 PM as boats stop running and security decreases. Only swim in designated areas; currents can be strong near the breakwater. 
Contact info: N/A (Boat operators at Fiki Marina or Mekwe Jetty) 
Available activities: Surfing (popular spot), swimming, volleyball, quad biking, relaxing.
Address / Access Info: Onisiwo Island (Tarkwa Bay island), off Lagos harbour; accessible only by boat / water taxi from Marina / Victoria Island / Lagos Island jetties.

7. Badagry Slave Route
Name: Badagry Heritage Museum & Slave Route 
State / City / LGA: Lagos State / Badagry / Badagry LGA 
Description: A collection of historical sites marking the Trans-Atlantic slave trade era. Includes the First Storey Building in Nigeria, Point of No Return, and the Heritage Museum.
 Entry fees: Total tour budget approx. ₦5,000 - ₦10,000 per person (covers entry to ~5 different small sites and the boat to Gberefu Island). 
Opening hours: Daily, 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM 
GPS coordinates: 6.4173° N, 2.8837° E 
Photos: 
History: Badagry was a major slave port. The "Point of No Return" is the path slaves walked to board ships. The First Storey Building (1845) is where the English Bible was translated to Yoruba.
 Best time to visit: Early morning start (it is a 2-3 hour drive from Lagos mainland). 
Safety info: The road to Badagry is currently under construction (as of 2024/25), so travel can be rough. Use a recognized tour guide. 
Contact info: Local guides usually available at the museum entrance.
 Available activities: Historical education, boat ride to Gberefu Island, walking the slave path, drinking from the "miracle well."
 Address / Access Info: 125 Marina Road, Badagry 103101, Lagos, Nigeria.

State / City / LGA: Lagos State / Marina, CMS / Lagos Island LGA 
Description: The oldest Anglican cathedral in the Church of Nigeria. Its Norman Gothic architecture and clock tower make it one of Lagos' most photographed landmarks. 
Entry fees: Free (It is a place of worship). 
Opening hours: Daily (Service times: Sunday mornings). 
GPS coordinates: 6.4539° N, 3.3900° E

Best time to visit: Sunday morning (to hear the choir) or Saturday (for exterior photography). 
Safety info: Marina is a busy business district. Exercise caution with cameras on the street. 
Contact info: +234 1 271 2855 
Available activities: Religious service, architectural appreciation, photography (exterior).
 Address / Access Info: 29 Marina Road, Lagos Island, Lagos 101001 / 102273, Nigeria.

20. Tafawa Balewa Square (TBS)
Name: Tafawa Balewa Square 
State / City / LGA: Lagos State / Lagos Island / Lagos Island LGA 
Description: A ceremonial ground with a distinctive entrance gate featuring giant horses and eagles. It was the site of Nigeria’s independence celebration in 1960. 
Entry fees: Free (to view exterior/square); Fees apply if entering for a trade fair or event. Opening hours: Daily (Business hours). 
GPS coordinates: 6.4453° N, 3.4012° E 
Photos: 
History: Originally a race course, it was reconstructed in 1972 and named after Nigeria’s first Prime Minister. It housed the National Assembly before the capital moved to Abuja. 
Best time to visit: Daytime. It is often used for the "Lagos International Trade Fair" (November) or "The Experience" concert (December). 
Safety info: Located in a very busy commercial hub. Watch out for "Area Boys" (street touts) in the surrounding bus stops. 
Contact info: N/A 
Available activities: Photography of the monuments, shopping (there is a shopping complex inside), attending major concerts.
Address / Access Info: 45/57 Massey Bamgboshe Street, Lagos Island, Onikan/Onikan-Race Course area, Lagos, Nigeria.
